Refrigeration Solution for office purposes

Designed a solution for shared refrigerator space for offices to increase efficiency in maintenance.

The Problem

Office refrigerators are unhygenic!

While it’s safe to say that disappointment from opening the work refrigerator to see the lunch you were looking forward to is now missing, or that someone has used all of your coffee creamer, or that the spill/rotting fruit from 3 weeks ago still isn’t cleaned, is not the biggest disappointment one will experience throughout life, it can still be a defining blow in an otherwise fine day. The office refrigerator has become so ubiquitous with being a workplace hazard that it’s a become a steady punchline in sitcoms and movies. Complaints tend to be the same regardless of industry, gender, city, or pay grade when everyone has to deal with the break room refrigerator.

Our Approach

The goal is to meet the emotional needs of employees above the commonly accepted basic need and functional element of storing food and beverages at work. To achieve this, we took a step-by-step approach to design a product.

Opportunity Understanding

We initially reached out to five of the target customers we had interviewed during our planning phase and had expressed interest in helping us design a product for their specific needs. Using interactive mockups I created with InVision, I interviewed both marketers and executives to explain our initial ideas. The primary goals were to design the analytics dashboard and the user flow to add and manage influencers.

Learnings and Improvements

An interesting learning after showing the mockups to customers was that they were confused by the scoring system proposed in the influencer ranking, dubbed the “LikeWallet score”. Although I had envisioned that feature as an an easy way to identify good and bad influencers (like a rotten tomatoes score for influencers!), It was clear that the customers would not blindly trust any scoring system without knowing how it was computed or how the data was collected. They wanted to have an active role in evaluating the performance of an influencer. For the next iteration, I replaced the percentage score with a more familiar 5-star rating, and changed the design to display the relevant statistics.

Improved Rating System

Another thing I learned quickly was that the needs of customers varied greatly across companies, even within the same industry. Therefore it was clear that a static design would not suit the needs of all our users. To solve this problem, I improved the modularity of the design by switching to a card-based design. I envisioned that this would provide greater flexibility to allow users to modify the layout of the dashboard to best fit their needs.

How We Got There

Designing and Testing User Flows

A key challenge of this project was designing an user flow to add influencers to LikeWallet and manage them. I started by using an interactive mockup to test a simple linear flow with our target users. The idea was that at the creation of a new campaign, the user would enter required information including the handles of the influencers and the amount that they would get compensated. The final step would require users to share a referral link with each influencer in order to track website traffic more accurately.

1. Create new campaign

2. Enter influencers

3. Enter investment information

4. Share referral links with influencers

Testing the flow with users revealed several areas of improvement. A key problem was that the flow did not meet the needs of users who used influencers on a regular basis. Instead of planning and running temporary campaigns, these marketeers partnered with influencers to have them continuously post throughout the year. Therefore they needed to add and manage influencers on the go, and not just at the start of a campaign.

To solve this issue, I designed a dedicated page to manage influencers, which could be accessed at all times via the main navigation bar.

Adding and deleting influencers

For users that did need to simultaneously manage campaigns with different influencers, I designed a feature to organize influencers into different groups. That way, on the dashboard, users can filter the analytics and data by group.

Creating groups of influencers

Another problem with the initial flow involved the final step in which the user was required to send a referral link to each of their influencers. This step was problematic because it required the user to take action outside or platform and also required a response from the actual influencer, which was something we could not control. This could create a disruption in our flow that would impact the user's experience negatively.

"We've worked with other companies to put special code and features on our website"

- Target Customer

After doing some technology research we determined that it would be possible to track website traffic driven by influencers without using a referral link. Instead, we would inject code into the customer's website to monitor internet traffic and correlate traffic spikes to posting times of influencer posts. Our initial fear with this approach was that customers would be reluctant to integrate such code into their website, but our interviews revealed that this was not the case.

How We Got There

Testing Market Demand

With our high-fidelity and functional prototype ready, our next step was to test the market demand for LikeWallet. We first gave the prototype to the target customers we had been interviewing since the beginning in order to evaluate their willingness to pay for the product. Following that, I designed a landing page and animated an explainer video to launch a campaign on betalist.com and test how many businesses would sign up.

"Could I get three more seat please?"

- Target Customer

The prototype was a great success with our first real users, who used it daily from the first day they got it. In fact, we started getting overwhelmed by the amount of feedback, feature requests and bug reports that we started receiving. Three companies asked if they could get more seats, and one of them offered to sign an annual contract of $10,000.

The betalist campaign was also successful. From our research, we had chosen to measure it's success by tracking the number of soft signups and setting a target of 50 after a week. The campaign ended up generating about 400 visits to the landing page, with 77 conversions to a sign up.

Next Steps

Influencer Search Engine

This project has shown that there is a clear need and demand for more robust analytics around influencer marketing. However, during our customer discovery phase we also identified other potential areas for opportunities within the space.

"Instagram is filled with wannabe influencers"

- Target Customer

For example, several customers expressed experiencing pain points while searching for new influencers to work with. There is a lack of tools to search for influencers, and it's extremely hard to know how well they will perform without running real trials. As a result, marketers are spending hours manually stalking social media profiles, looking for potential red flags such as bot followers and low engagement rates.

I recommend pursuing this opportunity to create an end-to-end platform for influencer marketing. Our vision of making influencer marketing accessible to all can only be achieved if we create tools to help companies find the best influencers for their brand.
