Bridgestone Americas, Inc.

I was a student corporate consultant for Bridgestone under Experential Learning Initiative by Krannert School of Management (Purdue). The R&D team at Ohio developed a new technology that could replace traditional air filled tires. Like many new products in technology, R&D comes before product management. The Research team wanted to present Airless tire technology to the business unit in Nashville and used ELI at Purdue to draw Product potential for Airless tires and for Bridgestone.

Roles and Achievements

• Designed mechanisms for a new AC power receptacle.
• Worked on high speed communication cables and power accessories.
• Used NX and ANSYS to model complex assembly drawings and conduct FEA.
• Designed complex plastic and metal parts to meet cosmetic, reliability and manufacturability standards.
• Optimized part and system assembly to create the most reliable and safe experience for the user.
• Created MCO and PCB designs to effectively integrate mechanical and electrical requirements.
• Drafted detailed sheet drawings using GD&T principles.
• Conducted Failure Analysis (FA) and modeled Design of Experiments (DOE) for specification testing.
